I work on human-centered AI, cognitive ergonomics, and multimodal sensing (e.g., eye tracking, physiology, interaction logs) to design practical, trustworthy systems for learning, health, and safety-critical operations.

My research examines how people coordinate with AI teammates under uncertainty— how attention, cognitive load, and interaction dynamics evolve over time— and how adaptive interfaces can support resilient performance in real-world contexts.

Research Interests

  • Human–AI teaming, adaptive decision support, trust & uncertainty
  • Nonlinear dynamics, time-series analysis, mixed models
  • Wearable sensing, stress & fatigue inference, cognitive state modeling

Selected Projects

  • mHelp / Digital Health — wearable stress detection & intervention support
  • Human–AI Teammates — adaptive HMI behaviors in supervisory control
  • Curiosity-Driven Learning — AI curiosity-regulation frameworks
